Why it's built this way. The categories you can't measure — off-the-books labor, "lost wages from Americans" — are deliberately left off as live counters, because a fabricated number would let critics discredit every real number beside it. The contested fiscal figure is shown as an estimate with its source named and the offsetting taxes-paid noted. That's what makes this version harder to attack than a site full of round, unsourced totals — and therefore more persuasive to a skeptic, a reporter, or a Congressional staffer.
